Kheer

Kheer is a rich, creamy rice pudding made with full-fat milk, fragrant basmati rice, and aromatic spices. This traditional dessert is especially popular during celebrations, weddings, and other special occasions throughout South Asia. The slow cooking process creates a deliciously thick consistency that's garnished with nuts and dried fruits.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 2 liters full-fat milk
  • 1 cup sugar (or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup khoya/mawa (milk solids) - optional for extra richness
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder (elaichi)
  • A pinch of saffron strands (kesar), soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk
  • 1/4 cup mixed nuts (almonds, pistachios, cashews), blanched and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons raisins
  • 1 tablespoon rose water (gulab jal)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2-3 whole green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  • Silver varq (silver leaf) for garnishing - optional

Method

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. After soaking, drain the water completely.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pot or karahi, heat the ghee and add the crushed cardamom pods. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the drained rice and sauté for 2-3 minutes on medium heat until it starts to look translucent but not browned.
  4. Pour in the milk and bring it to a boil, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. Once the milk comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer. Continue cooking uncovered, stirring frequently to prevent the milk from sticking to the bottom.
  6. Cook for approximately 1.5-2 hours until the rice is completely soft and the milk reduces to half its original quantity. The slow cooking is essential for developing the rich flavor of kheer.
  7. Add the sugar and mix well until it completely dissolves. If using khoya/mawa, add it at this stage and mix thoroughly.
  8. Add the cardamom powder, soaked saffron with its milk, and ground cinnamon. Stir to combine.
  9. Mix in half of the sliced nuts and all of the raisins.
  10. Continue to simmer for another 10-15 minutes until the kheer reaches a thick, creamy consistency (it will thicken further upon cooling).
  11. Remove from heat and stir in the rose water.
  12. Transfer to serving bowls. The kheer can be served warm, at room temperature, or chilled according to preference.
  13. Garnish with the remaining nuts and silver varq (if using) just before serving.
  14. For an authentic touch, serve with fresh naan or chapati during special occasions.
